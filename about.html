The Life of William Riley Brooksher

William Riley Brooksher was born in Turkey, Arkansas on February 10th, 1930. He grew up in a town with no more than a post office and a church at the time of his birth. At age 2, he would lose his sister, Juanita, and at age 11, his father. Such early hardships did not stop him from being a bright and ambitious young man. Whilst helping his mother scrape together a living, he would go on to become to be top of his class and class president at Yellville High School in Arkansas. Unsure of what to do with his life, he decided to enlist in the Air Force.


From there, he would go on to overcome every challenge the military put before him and would rise through the ranks. He would eventually become a Brigadier General before his retirement took him to Richland, Washington. During his time in the military, he would go back to school and receive his bachelor's degree from the University of Colorado as well as his master's in Business Administration.


After a life of adventure and moving from place to place, William Brooksher would go on to complete the lifelong dream of becoming an author. A man with an extensive library and a wealth of knowledge, he would go on to write about various battles in the Civil War, a subject he found particularly captivating. He would spend the last of his days spending time with the ones he loved, reading a good book and working on his latest great piece of literature.
